Smoked Rice with Holy Trinity and Corn

Serves 12-16 people • Prep Time: 20 minutes • Cook Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 6 cups long-grain white rice
  • 9-12 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 3 large onions, diced
  • 3 large bell peppers, diced
  • 8-10 celery stalks, diced
  • 4½ cups fresh or frozen corn kernels
  • 12 cloves garlic, minced
  • 9 tablespoons butter or oil
  • 6 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1½ teaspoons cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 9 green onions, chopped
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the holy trinity:
    You'll need a very large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven (at least 8-quart capacity). Heat butter or oil over medium heat. Add the diced onions, bell peppers, and celery. Cook for 12-15 minutes until vegetables are softened and lightly caramelized, stirring frequently.
  2. Add aromatics:
    Stir in minced garlic and cook for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Toast the rice:
    Add the rice to the pot and stir to coat with the vegetables and fat. Toast for 4-5 minutes until the rice is lightly golden, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Season and simmer:
    Add the stock, bay leaves, smoked paprika, cayenne, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes.
  5. Add corn:
    Stir in the corn kernels, cover again, and cook for another 8-10 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
  6. Finish:
    Remove from heat and let stand covered for 10 minutes. Remove bay leaves, fluff with a fork, and stir in chopped green onions.
  7. Serve:
    Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot.

Tips for Large Batch:

  • Use a 10-12 quart pot or divide between two large pots
  • You may need to cook in batches if your stovetop can't accommodate such a large pot
  • Start with 9 cups of stock and add more as needed - larger batches sometimes need extra liquid
  • Consider using two burners if your pot is very large to ensure even heating
  • Allow extra time for the larger volume to come to temperature

Note: The smoked paprika gives this dish that wonderful smoky flavor without needing an actual smoker. For extra smokiness, you can also add a small amount of liquid smoke to the stock.

This recipe serves 12-16 people as a generous side dish!
